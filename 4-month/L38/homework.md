## Questions

1. Explain the difference between `INNER JOIN` and `LEFT JOIN`. Give a real business example where using `INNER JOIN` would silently hide important data that `LEFT JOIN` would correctly include. Why might the wrong choice of JOIN type lead to incorrect business decisions?

2. Explain what a **Cartesian product** is and how it happens in SQL. Why is it dangerous, and what specific mistake causes it? If a table with 500 rows is accidentally Cartesian-joined to a table with 1,000 rows, how many rows does the result contain?

3. A **self JOIN** is when a table is joined to itself. Describe two different real-world data structures that require a self-join to query properly. For each one, explain what the `manager_id`-style column represents and what question the self-join answers.

4. In normalized database design, data is split across many tables connected by foreign keys. Explain the trade-off: what does normalization give you (the benefits), and what does it cost you (the added complexity)? How do JOINs bridge this gap?
