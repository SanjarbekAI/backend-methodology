## Questions

1. Python has two division operators: `/` (regular division) and `//` (floor division). Explain the difference between them with your own words, and describe a real situation where each one would be the right choice. For example, when would you need a decimal result, and when would you need a whole number?

2. The modulo operator `%` is one that beginners often find confusing or useless at first. Explain what it actually does, and then give two concrete real-world examples where modulo would be genuinely useful in a program — not a math textbook problem, but a real system or app.

3. Type casting converts a value from one type to another. Describe what happens step by step when a user types their age into a form on a website, and the program needs to compare it to a minimum age requirement. Why is casting necessary, and what could go wrong if it is not done?

4. Compare the `and`, `or`, and `not` logical operators. Give a real-life scenario (such as a login system, a ticket booking app, or a delivery tracker) that requires combining at least two conditions, and explain which operator you would use and why.
