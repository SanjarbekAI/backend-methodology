## Questions

1. A dictionary stores data as key-value pairs and requires that all keys are unique. Explain why key uniqueness is essential for a dictionary to work correctly. What would happen to a lookup system (like a phone contact book or a product catalog) if duplicate keys were allowed?

2. Sets and lists can both store collections of items, but they are fundamentally different. Compare sets and lists across at least four characteristics (ordering, duplicates, performance, use cases). Then describe a real-world scenario where you would specifically choose a set over a list and explain why.

3. The `.get()` method is considered safer than direct `dict[key]` access. Explain the difference between the two approaches, describe what happens when the key doesn't exist in each case, and give a real example of a situation where using `.get()` would prevent a crash.

4. Nested dictionaries allow you to represent complex, structured data. Think of a real-world system — such as a hospital patient database, a school roster, or a company's employee directory — and describe what that data would look like as a nested dictionary. What are the benefits of this structure compared to using separate variables?
