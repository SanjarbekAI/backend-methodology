## Questions

1. Python uses indentation instead of curly braces `{}` to define blocks of code. Why do you think the creators of Python made this choice? What are the advantages and disadvantages of this approach compared to languages that use `{}`?

2. Compare single-line comments (`#`) and multi-line strings (`"""..."""`) used as comments. When would you choose one over the other, and why? Give a real example of a situation where each one is more appropriate.

3. The `print()` function seems very simple — it just shows text on the screen. But think about a real application like a banking app or an e-commerce website. Describe at least three different ways output (showing information to a user) plays an important role in that kind of system.

4. Imagine you are working on a team of five developers. You write 200 lines of code and submit it with zero comments. Describe what problems this could cause for your teammates. What kinds of things would you comment, and what would you leave uncommented?

5. Python reads code from top to bottom. Describe a real-life situation — not a programming one — that works the same way: the order of steps matters and cannot be changed. Explain the consequences of doing the steps in the wrong order.
